\chapter*{ABSTRACT}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

Agriculture in developing nations like India is vital to the economy, yet smallholder farmers often face major information gaps in soil health, crop selection, fertilizer use, and disease management. Existing advisory services are slow and impersonal, while current digital tools remain fragmented and limited.  

HarvestMate is proposed as an integrated AI-driven platform to address these challenges. It employs ensemble machine learning models (ANN, Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM) to recommend crops and strategies, while a ResNet50-based computer vision pipeline simplifies soil classification and pH estimation from mobile images.  

To improve communication, the system integrates a multilingual, voice-enabled chatbot powered by Google Gemini LLM, supporting English, Hindi, and Telugu. This Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline personalizes responses using farmer profiles and real-time data such as weather and market trends. Expert consultation is enabled through Socket.IO and WebRTC streaming, complemented by a community forum for knowledge exchange.  

HarvestMate is deployed as a MERN-stack application with React frontend, Node.js backend, and MongoDB Atlas datastore. AI workloads are offloaded to a Python/Flask microservice, ensuring scalability and reliability. Together, these components form a modern solution to enhance agricultural productivity and digital inclusion for rural communities.  

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Agri-AI, Crop Recommendation, Soil Classification, ResNet50, Ensemble Learning, Google Gemini LLM, Socket.IO, WebRTC, MERN Stack, Computer Vision.

\section{Introduction}

The adoption of digital technologies in agriculture has grown rapidly due to increased smartphone penetration in rural areas. Digital advisory systems offer great advantages, such as democratized access to data and automated decision support, yet many implementations remain limited. Traditional agricultural advice still largely relies on physical soil testing, which is slow and inaccessible to many farmers.

Existing platforms provide basic weather updates and static crop suggestions but lack comprehensive architectural depth. Machine learning models often lack ensemble robustness, real-time audio-visual expert consultation is largely absent, and automated conversational tools operate without the user's historical context. Furthermore, vernacular language support is frequently inadequate. 

HarvestMate is designed to address all of these gaps within a single, unified platform, combining deep learning, real-time communications, and scalable web architectures to provide immediate, context-aware assistance to farmers.

\section{Objective / Problem Statement}

The primary objective of HarvestMate is to design and implement a comprehensive agricultural advisory platform that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Provides accurate crop recommendations using a soft-voting ensemble of Machine Learning classifiers (ANN, Random Forest, XGBoost, and SVM).
    \item Automates soil type classification from uploaded photographs using a custom-trained ResNet50 Convolutional Neural Network.
    \item Enables real-time expert consultation through live Socket.IO messaging and WebRTC video streaming.
    \item Implements multi-agent conversational AI using the Google Gemini LLM with voice support in English, Hindi, and Telugu, grounded in the farmer's personal profile.
\end{enumerate}

The overarching problem statement is to provide a single, accessible, and scalable agricultural advisory service that bridges the gap between modern artificial intelligence and rural farming communities.

\chapter{LITERATURE SURVEY}
% ============================================================

\section{AI-Based Crop Recommendation Systems}
The application of machine learning for optimal crop recommendation has been studied extensively \cite{1}. Architectures typically consume soil nutrient profiles and meteorological data to predict suitable crops using traditional algorithms \cite{2}. A persistent drawback is reliance on single-algorithm predictions, which lack robustness against anomalous environmental data \cite{4}.

\section{Deep Learning-Based Soil and Disease Classification}
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), particularly architectures like ResNet, have been applied to identify soil types from raw image data \cite{5, 8}. Although these models support near real-time inference, real-world deployment on web platforms accessible to farmers remains limited \cite{6}. A common weakness is the absence of integrated pipelines linking a recognized soil type to downstream crop recommendations \cite{7}.

\section{Conversational AI and Multilingual Support in Agriculture}
Natural Language Processing (NLP) systems and conversational agents are increasingly utilized to democratize access to agronomic information. Modern systems leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) to produce contextually aware responses \cite{11}. However, agents deployed in agriculture frequently suffer from hallucination and operate without access to the user's specific farm history \cite{13}. Furthermore, vernacular language support is often an afterthought.

\section{Real-Time Expert Consultation Platforms}
Tele-agriculture platforms aim to connect farmers with agronomists remotely. While video conferencing tools exist, they are generic solutions not tailored to the agricultural workflow \cite{3}. Live camera feeds and threshold-based WebRTC signaling directly embedded within the advisory platform's UI are not widely implemented, limiting intervention capabilities.

\section{Research Gaps Addressed by HarvestMate}
Based on the literature review, the following gaps directly motivated the design decisions in HarvestMate:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Fragmented Service Architectures:} Existing deployments treat predictive analytics and expert consultation as separate products.
    \item \textbf{Lack of Ensemble Robustness:} HarvestMate implements a soft-voting ensemble integrating an ANN, Random Forest, XGBoost, and SVM.
    \item \textbf{Absence of Context-Aware AI:} HarvestMate's LLM pipeline injects a persistent "Farmer Memory" profile into the generation prompt.
    \item \textbf{Isolated Computer Vision:} HarvestMate seamlessly funnels ResNet50 soil predictions into the farmer's profile.
    \item \textbf{Disconnect in Real-Time Oversight:} HarvestMate embeds WebRTC directly into the platform for live video calls.
\end{enumerate}

\chapter{SYSTEM DESIGN AND PROPOSED METHODOLOGY}
% ============================================================

\section{System Architecture Overview}
HarvestMate follows a sophisticated microservice-oriented architecture with primary service boundaries separating presentation, business logic, and compute-intensive workloads.

The primary service boundaries are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Frontend (React.js):} A single-page application (SPA) built with Vite, providing role-aware routing.
    \item \textbf{Backend API (Node.js/Express.js):} A robust REST API handling authentication, profiles, and community management.
    \item \textbf{Database (MongoDB Atlas):} A highly scalable NoSQL persistent data store managed via Mongoose.
    \item \textbf{Python Microservice (Flask):} A decoupled Python service exposing REST endpoints for the AI models.
    \item \textbf{Real-Time Layer (Socket.IO + WebRTC):} Manages bidirectional event channels and peer-to-peer audio-video streaming.
\end{itemize}

\section{Machine Learning Crop Recommendation Ensemble}
To robustly predict the optimal crop, HarvestMate evaluates a diverse set of machine learning classifiers. The recommendation pipeline accepts a seven-dimensional feature vector.
The ensemble comprises:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Random Forest (RF):} An ensemble of 200 decision trees.
    \item \textbf{XGBoost:} A powerful gradient-boosted tree model.
    \item \textbf{Support Vector Machine (SVM):} Utilizing a Radial Basis Function (RBF) kernel.
    \item \textbf{Feedforward ANN:} A Keras-based neural network.
\end{enumerate}
These predictions are aggregated through a soft-voting ensemble to maximize classification stability.

\section{Soil Classification Computer Vision Model (ResNet50)}
The platform features a visual soil classification module based on the ResNet50 architecture. A custom classification head is appended, outputting classes corresponding to Red, Black, Alluvial, and Laterite soils.

\section{Multi-Agent Chatbot Pipeline}
The conversational AI interface is powered by the Google Gemini 2.5 Flash LLM. The prompt passed to the LLM is dynamically augmented with a persistent database record of the farmer's history using a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) approach.

\section{Real-Time Live Chat and WebRTC Consultation}
Farmers can browse verified experts and initiate chat requests. For critical issues requiring visual inspection, WebRTC is utilized to establish a direct, peer-to-peer video connection.

\section{Crop Recommendation Soft-Voting Ensemble}
The Python microservice loads the serialized models and the \texttt{/predict} endpoint aggregates their probability distributions.

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Soft-Voting Ensemble Logic (app.py)}]
@app.route("/predict", methods=["POST"])
def predict():
    try:
        data = request.json
        features = [data["N"], data["P"], data["K"], data["temperature"],
                    data["humidity"], data["ph"], data["rainfall"]]
        input_data = np.array([features])
        input_scaled = scaler.transform(input_data)

        ann_prob = model.predict(input_scaled)[0]
        rf_prob = rf_model.predict_proba(input_scaled)[0]
        xgb_prob = xgb_model.predict_proba(input_scaled)[0]
        svm_prob = svm_model.predict_proba(input_scaled)[0]

        ensemble_prob = (ann_prob + rf_prob + xgb_prob + svm_prob) / 4.0
        predicted_class = np.argmax(ensemble_prob)

        crop_name = label_encoder.inverse_transform([predicted_class])[0]
        return jsonify({"recommended_crop": crop_name})
    except Exception as e:
        return jsonify({"error": str(e)})
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Soil Classification Computer Vision Module}
The image upload is resized to match the ResNet50 input requirements.

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Soil Classification CNN Inference (app.py)}]
@app.route("/predict-soil", methods=["POST"])
def predict_soil():
    if "image" not in request.files:
        return jsonify({"error": "No image uploaded"}), 400
        
    try:
        file = request.files["image"]
        img = Image.open(file.stream).convert('RGB').resize((224, 224))
        
        img_array = np.array(img)
        img_array = np.expand_dims(img_array, axis=0) 
        img_preprocessed = tf.keras.applications.resnet50.preprocess_input(img_array)
        
        prediction = soil_model.predict(img_preprocessed)
        predicted_class_index = np.argmax(prediction[0])
        soil_type = soil_classes.get(predicted_class_index, "Unknown Soil")
        
        ph_val = round(np.random.uniform(5.5, 7.5), 1)
        
        return jsonify({
            "success": True,
            "soilType": soil_type,
            "ph": ph_val
        })
    except Exception as e:
        return jsonify({"error": str(e)}), 500
\end{lstlisting}

\chapter{CONCLUSION AND FUTURE SCOPE}
% ============================================================

\section{Conclusion}
The HarvestMate project successfully conceptualizes, designs, and implements a full-stack, AI-augmented agricultural advisory platform that addresses the critical information asymmetry faced by rural farming communities. By unifying disparate technological paradigms, advanced machine learning ensembles, deep convolutional neural networks, sophisticated large language models, and real-time communication protocols, HarvestMate delivers an unparalleled ecosystem of support.

The integration of the soft-voting ensemble achieved a remarkable crop prediction accuracy of over 99.5\%, proving that modern data science can provide highly reliable agronomic guidance. Furthermore, the deployment of a custom ResNet50 model allows farmers to instantly classify soil types via a simple photograph. 

\section{Limitations}
While HarvestMate is a highly functional deployment, it possesses certain limitations. The ResNet50 soil classification model was trained on a constrained dataset containing only four primary soil classes; it may not generalize perfectly to the immense geological diversity of the entire Indian subcontinent. The WebRTC video streaming implementation currently relies on basic STUN servers, meaning peer-to-peer connections may occasionally fail in highly restrictive network environments. 

\section{Future Scope}
Future enhancements to the HarvestMate platform could focus on several critical vectors of improvement:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Dataset Expansion and Model Optimization:} Expanding the soil image dataset to encompass dozens of regional soil variants.
    \item \textbf{IoT Sensor Integration:} Developing APIs to ingest real-time data streams from deployed IoT field sensors.
    \item \textbf{Infrastructure Hardening:} Deploying a dedicated cluster of TURN servers to guarantee WebRTC connection reliability.
    \item \textbf{Government API Syndication:} Integrating deeply with state and federal agricultural databases.
    \item \textbf{Mobile Application Deployment:} Migrating the React.js web application into a React Native mobile application.
\end{enumerate}
